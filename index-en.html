About me

I hold a Master’s degree in Natural Language Processing (2024), where my research focused on morphosemantics and derivational morphology through the computational analysis of Russian suffixes and the digitization of a specialized dictionary. This work allowed me to develop expertise in derivational morphology, morphosyntactic analysis, and distributional representations (word embeddings). I also gained practical experience in applied NLP (SpaCy, NLTK, Transformers) during a data science internship. I am eager to continue this line of research in a PhD, contributing to computational models of word formation with a multilingual perspective.
